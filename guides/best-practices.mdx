## Overview

Trellixe is a powerful networking CRM designed to help you build and maintain meaningful professional relationships. This guide shares best practices for getting the most value from Trellixe, from initial setup to advanced workflow optimization.

## Getting Started Right

### Initial Setup Checklist

Set yourself up for success from day one:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete Your Profile">
    - Add your full name for professional appearance
    - Verify your email address immediately
    - Set your correct timezone for accurate reminders
    - Upload a profile photo (when available)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Web Push Notifications">
    - Grant browser notification permission
    - Test notifications on all devices
    - Configure notification preferences
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Your First Workspace">
    - Use descriptive names ("Tech Conferences" not "Workspace 1")
    - Decide if personal or team workspace
    - Set up workspace structure before adding data
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Import Initial Contacts">
    - Start with recent networking events
    - Add context notes immediately while fresh
    - Tag contacts by category or event
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Complete setup within 24 hours of signing up while your momentum is high. Users who finish setup early are 3x more likely to become active long-term.
</Tip>

## Contact Management Best Practices

### Adding Contacts

**When to add contacts:**
- Immediately after meeting someone new
- While business cards are still in your pocket
- Before leaving a networking event
- Within 24 hours at the latest

**What information to capture:**
- Full name (exactly as they prefer)
- Email address (primary contact method)
- Company and role
- Where you met (event context)
- Key conversation points
- Mutual connections
- Follow-up commitments

<Warning>
  Don't wait until "later" to add contacts. The longer you wait, the more context you lose and the less likely you'll follow up effectively.
</Warning>

### Organizing Contacts

**Use workspaces strategically:**
- Separate personal and professional contacts
- Create workspaces by industry or interest area
- Use team workspaces for company networking

**Contact categorization:**
- Tag by event attended
- Group by industry or role
- Mark priority contacts for frequent follow-up
- Note relationship strength (new, warm, strong)

<Accordion title="Contact Organization Strategies">
  **By Event:**
  - "TechCrunch Disrupt 2026"
  - "Local JavaScript Meetup"
  - "Industry Conference Q1"
  
  **By Industry:**
  - "SaaS Founders"
  - "Marketing Professionals"
  - "Investors & VCs"
  
  **By Priority:**
  - "Hot Leads" - immediate follow-up
  - "Warm Connections" - regular check-ins
  - "General Network" - occasional updates
</Accordion>

### Contact Data Quality

**Maintain clean data:**
- Use consistent naming formats
- Verify email addresses (watch for typos)
- Update information as relationships evolve
- Remove duplicate entries
- Archive inactive contacts rather than deleting

<Note>
  High-quality contact data is the foundation of effective networking. Spend extra time ensuring accuracy when adding contacts.
</Note>

## Event Management Strategies

### Creating Events

**Event setup best practices:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Events Before Attending">
    Set up the event in Trellixe before you attend, so you're ready to add contacts in real-time
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Include Complete Event Details">
    - Full event name
    - Date and location
    - Event type/category
    - Your attendance goals
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Prepare Invitation Strategy">
    - Decide who you want to meet
    - Set networking goals (quantity and quality)
    - Plan follow-up timeline
  </Step>
</Steps>

### During Events

**Real-time contact capture:**
1. Open Trellixe on your mobile device
2. Add contacts immediately after each conversation
3. Note key discussion points while fresh
4. Mark follow-up commitments
5. Rate conversation quality/priority

<Tip>
  Take 30 seconds after each meaningful conversation to add the contact to Trellixe. This small habit dramatically increases follow-up success rates.
</Tip>

**Event networking workflow:**
- Set a goal (e.g., "make 10 quality connections")
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Add context notes immediately
- Take photos of business cards for reference
- Schedule follow-up tasks before leaving event

### Post-Event Follow-Up

**The 24-hour rule:**
- Send initial follow-up within 24 hours
- Reference specific conversation points
- Provide promised resources immediately
- Schedule next interaction

**Follow-up timeline:**
- **Day 1:** Initial connection email/message
- **Week 1:** Value-add follow-up (article, intro, resource)
- **Week 2-4:** Check-in or meeting invite
- **Month 2-3:** Quarterly check-in cadence

<Warning>
  The fortune is in the follow-up. 80% of networking value comes from consistent follow-up, not the initial meeting.
</Warning>

## Follow-Up Task Management

### Creating Effective Follow-Up Tasks

**Task creation best practices:**

<Accordion title="Immediate Follow-Up (0-24 hours)">
  - Send promised resources
  - Make introductions you committed to
  - Share contact information
  - Send thank you message
  
  **Example task:** "Send Sarah the marketing report I mentioned + LinkedIn connection request"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Short-Term Follow-Up (1-7 days)">
  - Schedule coffee or video call
  - Share relevant article or resource
  - Ask thoughtful question
  - Provide helpful introduction
  
  **Example task:** "Send John article about AI in healthcare + suggest coffee next week"
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Long-Term Follow-Up (2-12 weeks)">
  - Quarterly check-in messages
  - Share relevant opportunities
  - Congratulate on achievements
  - Re-engage dormant connections
  
  **Example task:** "Congratulate Lisa on new role at Microsoft (saw on LinkedIn)"
</Accordion>

### Follow-Up Task Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Task Immediately">
    Don't wait - create follow-up tasks while context is fresh
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Realistic Due Dates">
    Be honest about your capacity. Better to set a realistic date and hit it than miss aggressive deadlines
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Web Push Notifications">
    Let Trellixe remind you so follow-ups don't slip through cracks
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Log Interaction Immediately">
    When you complete a follow-up, log the interaction right away with outcome notes
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Schedule Next Touch Point">
    Before marking a task complete, schedule the next follow-up
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Trellixe's web push notifications ensure you never miss a follow-up deadline. The notification includes contact name and event context to jog your memory.
</Note>

### Interaction Logging

**What to log:**
- Date and type of interaction (email, call, meeting, etc.)
- Key discussion points
- Commitments made (by you or them)
- Relationship status change
- Next steps decided

**Why logging matters:**
- Maintains relationship history
- Prevents redundant conversations
- Shows interaction patterns over time
- Helps team members collaborate
- Provides context for future interactions

<Tip>
  Spend 2 minutes logging interactions immediately after they occur. This documentation becomes invaluable months later when you can't remember previous conversations.
</Tip>

**Role assignment strategy:**
- **Admin:** Leadership team members who manage workspace
- **Member:** Active team members who add/edit contacts
- **Viewer:** Executives who need visibility without editing

**Collaboration workflows:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Establish Naming Conventions">
    Agree on consistent naming formats for contacts, events, and tasks across team
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Define Territory">
    Assign contact ownership to prevent duplicate follow-ups
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Share Context">

  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Coordinate Follow-Ups">
    Review follow-up tasks in team meetings to ensure coverage
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Without clear ownership, team contacts can fall through the cracks. Assign a primary owner for each important contact relationship.
</Warning>

## Notification Management

### Optimal Notification Setup

**Web push notifications to enable:**
- ✅ Follow-up task reminders (critical)
- ✅ Team member activities (if team workspace)
- ✅ Important contact updates

**Notification timing:**
- Set task reminders for times you can actually act
- Adjust browser notification settings per device
- Enable on desktop for work hours
- Enable on mobile for after-hours reminders

<Tip>
  Enable web push on both desktop and mobile. Desktop notifications during work hours, mobile notifications for evening/weekend follow-ups.
</Tip>

### Notification Workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Receive Notification">
    Web push notification appears with contact name and context
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Through">
    Click notification to open Trellixe directly to the task
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete Action">
    Execute the follow-up (send email, make call, etc.)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Log Interaction">
    Record what you did and the outcome immediately
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Schedule Next Touch">
    Before closing, schedule the next follow-up task
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Notification best practices:**
- Review notification center daily
- Mark notifications as read after acting
- Use "Mark All as Read" weekly to clear noise
- Adjust notification preferences if overwhelmed

## Security & Privacy Best Practices

### Account Security

**Password hygiene:**
- Use 20+ character passwords (longer is better)
- Unique password for Trellixe (never reuse)
- Consider password manager integration
- Change password if you suspect compromise

**Session management:**
- Review active sessions monthly
- Sign out from devices you no longer use
- Use "Sign Out All Other Sessions" if suspicious activity
- Enable web push for security notifications (when available)

<Warning>
  Your Trellixe account contains sensitive contact information. Treat security seriously to protect your network's privacy.
</Warning>

### Data Privacy

**Contact privacy considerations:**
- Only add contact information you have permission to store
- Be mindful of what you share in team workspaces
- Use privacy-respecting notes (avoid sensitive personal info)
- Review workspace member access regularly
- Remove team members promptly when they leave

**Workspace access control:**
- Assign minimum required permissions
- Use Viewer role for stakeholders who need visibility only
- Audit admin access regularly
- Don't share workspace passwords/sessions

<Note>
  In team workspaces, all members can see all contacts. Use separate workspaces for contacts that should remain private.
</Note>

</Accordion>

## Advanced Strategies

### Contact Relationship Stages

Manage contacts through relationship lifecycle:

**Stage 1: New Connection (0-30 days)**
- Frequency: Weekly follow-ups
- Goal: Establish relationship foundation
- Actions: Share value, find common ground, schedule meeting

**Stage 2: Active Relationship (1-6 months)**
- Frequency: Bi-weekly to monthly follow-ups
- Goal: Deepen relationship and mutual value
- Actions: Regular check-ins, share opportunities, make introductions

**Stage 3: Established Relationship (6+ months)**
- Frequency: Monthly to quarterly follow-ups
- Goal: Maintain connection and long-term value
- Actions: Quarterly check-ins, celebrate milestones, provide support

**Stage 4: Dormant (6+ months no contact)**
- Frequency: Quarterly re-engagement attempts
- Goal: Revive valuable relationships or archive
- Actions: Thoughtful re-engagement, update on your progress, show continued interest

<Note>
  Not all contacts need to progress through all stages. Some relationships naturally stabilize at earlier stages, and that's perfectly fine.
</Note>

### Event ROI Tracking

Measure networking event effectiveness:

**Metrics to track:**
- Number of contacts made
- Quality of conversations (subjective rating)
- Follow-up completion rate
- Meetings scheduled from event
- Opportunities generated
- Long-term relationships formed

**ROI calculation:**
1. Track event cost (ticket, travel, time)
2. Monitor contacts added from event
3. Measure follow-ups completed
4. Track opportunities created
5. Calculate value of relationships formed

<Tip>
  Use Trellixe's event-based contact organization to easily track all contacts from a specific event and measure that event's long-term networking value.
</Tip>

### Networking Automation

Automate where appropriate, personalize where it matters:

**Automate:**
- Follow-up task creation (immediately after meeting)
- Reminder notifications (web push)
- Session security monitoring
- Data backup and sync

**Never automate:**
- Actual follow-up messages (always personalize)
- Relationship decisions (archive vs. re-engage)
- Event note-taking (capture unique context)
- Interaction logging (document real conversations)

<Warning>
  Automation should support your networking, not replace it. People can tell when messages are templated. Always personalize actual communication.
</Warning>

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Delayed Contact Entry

**Problem:** Waiting days or weeks to add contacts to Trellixe

**Solution:** Add contacts within 1 hour of meeting them, ideally immediately

**Why it matters:** Context fades fast. Details you remember at the event are forgotten by the next day.

### Mistake 2: Follow-Up Procrastination

**Problem:** Creating follow-up tasks but not actually completing them

**Solution:** 
- Set realistic due dates
- Enable web push notifications
- Schedule follow-up time in your calendar
- Start with smaller, easier follow-ups to build momentum

### Mistake 3: Generic Follow-Ups

**Problem:** Sending "just checking in" messages without value

**Solution:**
- Always include specific context from your conversation
- Share something valuable (article, intro, resource)
- Ask thoughtful questions based on their interests
- Reference previous interactions

<Warning>
  Generic "touching base" messages get ignored. Your follow-up should give the recipient a specific reason to respond.
</Warning>

### Mistake 4: Contact Overload

**Problem:** Meeting 100 people at an event but not following up with any

**Solution:**
- Focus on quality over quantity
- Set realistic goals (10 quality connections vs. 100 business cards)
- Prioritize contacts immediately after event
- Follow up with top 10-20% first, others later

### Mistake 5: Neglecting Data Quality

**Problem:** Incomplete contact information, typos, duplicates

**Solution:**
- Spend extra 30 seconds ensuring accuracy when adding contacts
- Verify email addresses
- Use consistent naming formats
- Deduplicate contacts regularly
- Update information as it changes

### Mistake 6: Inconsistent Follow-Up Cadence

**Problem:** Following up 10 times in 2 weeks, then going silent for 6 months

**Solution:**
- Establish consistent follow-up rhythm per relationship stage
- Use Trellixe's follow-up tasks to maintain cadence
- Schedule next follow-up before completing current one
- Adjust frequency based on relationship depth

<Note>
  Consistency matters more than frequency. Better to follow up every 6 weeks reliably than sporadically with no pattern.
</Note>

## Measuring Success

### Key Networking Metrics

Track these metrics monthly:

**Input Metrics (what you control):**
- Events attended
- Contacts added
- Follow-ups completed
- Follow-up completion rate
- Average time to first follow-up

**Output Metrics (results):**
- Meetings scheduled
- Opportunities created
- Introductions made/received
- Relationships deepened
- Long-term value generated

**Quality Metrics:**
- Depth of relationships (how many strong connections)
- Network diversity (industries, roles, backgrounds)
- Relationship longevity (how many 1+ year connections)
- Mutual value exchange (give vs. receive ratio)
