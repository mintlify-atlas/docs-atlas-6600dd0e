Interaction logging in Trellixe creates a permanent record of your conversations and touchpoints with contacts. This relationship history helps you maintain continuity, personalize future interactions, and measure the effectiveness of your networking efforts.

## What is an Interaction Log?

An interaction log is a record that captures:

- **Contact** - Who you interacted with
- **User** - Which team member had the interaction
- **Follow-up Task** - The task that prompted this interaction
- **Event Context** - The event that led to this follow-up (through the task relationship)
- **Note** - Detailed description of what happened (required)
- **Timestamp** - When the log was created

Each log is associated with a follow-up task, creating a chain from event attendance → task → interaction log that preserves full context.

## Creating Interaction Logs

Interaction logs are typically created by completing follow-up tasks:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access Follow-up Task">
    Navigate to your Follow-up Tasks page from the main navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Task to Complete">
    Click the "Log Follow-up" button for the task you want to complete.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Context">
    On the logging form, review the provided context:
    - **How you met** - Information from the contact's profile
    - **Event notes** - Any notes added when they were marked as attended
    
    This context helps you personalize your outreach before making contact.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Make Contact">
    Call, email, or message the contact using the information in their profile.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Document the Interaction">
    In the "How did it go?" text area, record:
    - Whether you reached them and how (call, email, etc.)
    - What was discussed
    - Their responses or reactions
    - Any follow-up actions agreed upon
    - Next steps or opportunities identified
    - Personal details to remember for future interactions
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Complete">
    Click "Save Log and Complete Task" to:
    - Create the interaction log
    - Mark the follow-up task as complete
    - Add the log to the contact's history
    - Remove the task from your queue
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The note field is required. You cannot create an interaction log without documenting what happened. This ensures your relationship history remains meaningful and useful.
</Note>

## Atomic Task Completion

Interaction logs and task completion happen together in a database transaction:

```ruby
ActiveRecord::Base.transaction do
  @interaction_log.save!
  @follow_up_task.update!(completed_at: Time.current)
end
```

This ensures:
- Either both actions succeed, or neither does
- You cannot complete a task without logging the interaction
- Data consistency is maintained
- The relationship between logs and tasks is always valid

<Warning>
If the interaction log fails validation (missing note), the task will NOT be marked complete, and you'll see an error message. Both operations must succeed together.
</Warning>

## Viewing Interaction Logs

Interaction logs appear in multiple places throughout Trellixe:

### Contact Profile Page

The most comprehensive view is on the contact's profile:

1. Navigate to any contact's profile page
2. Click on the "Interaction History" tab
3. View all logged interactions with this contact, showing:
   - User avatar and name who logged the interaction
   - Date and time of the log
   - Event that led to this follow-up
   - Full interaction notes
   - Time elapsed since the interaction ("3 days ago")

Logs are displayed chronologically with the most recent at the top.

### Event Detail Page

Events show follow-up activity resulting from that event:

1. Navigate to any event's detail page
2. Scroll to the "Follow-up Activity" section
3. View all interactions logged for follow-ups from this event
4. Each entry shows:
   - Team member who completed the follow-up
   - Contact they followed up with (linked to profile)
   - Interaction notes
   - How long ago it occurred

This view helps you measure the ROI and relationship outcomes from specific events.

### Follow-up Task Detail

While completing a task, you can see:
- Previous event notes that were added when the contact was marked as attended
- Contact context (how you met)
- Link to the contact's full profile

## Interaction Log Associations

Understanding the data relationships helps you use the system effectively:

```
Event
  ↓ has_many
Invitation (links Event ↔ Contact)
  ↓ has_many
FollowUpTask
  ↓ has_many
InteractionLog
```

Each interaction log is connected to:
- **Contact** (directly) - Who you interacted with
- **User** (directly) - Who logged the interaction
- **FollowUpTask** (directly) - The task this log completes
- **Event** (through task → invitation) - The originating event
- **Invitation** (through task) - The specific invitation that created the task

This chain preserves full context for every interaction.

## What to Include in Interaction Notes

Write notes that will be valuable when you or a teammate interacts with this contact again:

<Accordion title="Good Interaction Note Examples">
**Example 1: Successful Call**
```
Called Sarah on 3/15. Had a great 15-minute conversation. She's interested in learning more about our product for her team. We discussed their current challenges with manual data entry and she sees value in automation. Scheduled a demo for 3/22 at 2pm. She mentioned she's training for a marathon - good personal connection point.
```

**Example 2: Email Follow-up**
```
Sent follow-up email with the resource we discussed at the event. Got a quick reply thanking me. He shared the doc with his co-founder and said they'll review it before our next quarterly meetup. No immediate action needed, but good continued rapport.
```

**Example 3: Voicemail**
```
Left voicemail at 555-0123. Mentioned we met at the Q1 dinner and I wanted to follow up on his question about integration options. Asked him to call back or email when convenient. Will try again in 3 days if I don't hear back.
```
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What to Avoid">
**Too brief:**
```
Called them.
```

**No outcome recorded:**
```
Talked about stuff.
```

**Missing next steps:**
```
Good conversation about the product.
```

These notes don't provide enough context for future interactions or for other team members.
</Accordion>

## Interaction Log Persistence

Interaction logs are permanent records:

- **Not editable** - Once created, logs cannot be modified (preserving accurate history)
- **Not deletable individually** - Logs remain as long as the contact exists
- **Cascade deletion** - Logs are automatically deleted if:
  - The contact is deleted (`has_many :interaction_logs, dependent: :destroy` on Contact)
  - The follow-up task is deleted (`has_many :interaction_logs, dependent: :destroy` on FollowUpTask)

<Warning>
Deleting a contact or follow-up task will permanently remove associated interaction logs. These deletions cannot be undone. Export or back up important relationship history before deleting contacts.
</Warning>

## Searching Interaction History

While Trellixe doesn't currently provide full-text search across interaction logs, you can:

1. **Filter by contact** - View all interactions with a specific person on their profile page
2. **Filter by event** - See all follow-up activity from a specific event on its detail page
3. **Filter by date** - Use event date filters to find interactions from a specific time period
4. **Filter by user** - Ask your administrator about queries to find all logs created by a specific team member

## Team Collaboration

Interaction logs enable effective team collaboration:

### Visibility

- All team members can view all interaction logs for contacts in your account
- Logs clearly show which user created them (with avatar and name)
- This transparency prevents duplicate outreach

### Continuity

When a teammate needs to interact with a contact:

1. They review the interaction history on the contact's profile
2. They see what's already been discussed
3. They understand the relationship stage and context
4. They can continue the conversation naturally without redundancy

### Accountability

Interaction logs create accountability:

- Managers can see follow-up activity from events
- Teams can measure networking ROI
- Individual contributions are tracked and visible

## Best Practices

<Accordion title="Log Immediately">
Create interaction logs right after the interaction while details are fresh. Don't wait days or weeks, as you'll forget important nuances that make notes valuable.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Be Specific">
Include specific details:
- Names of tools, products, or people mentioned
- Dates of scheduled next steps
- Direct quotes if particularly meaningful
- Personal details shared (appropriate ones)
- Outcomes or decisions made
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Write for Your Team">
Remember that others will read these notes. Write clearly and include context that helps teammates understand the relationship state, not just what you personally remember.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Note Unsuccessful Attempts">
Log when you can't reach someone too:
- Left voicemail
- Sent email with no response
- Wrong number

This helps prevent duplicate attempts and informs strategy for future outreach.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Include Next Steps">
Always end your note with next steps:
- "Scheduled demo for [date]"
- "Will try again in 1 week"
- "Waiting for them to email with availability"
- "No further action needed"

This helps you and your team know what to expect.
</Accordion>

## Privacy and Data Considerations

When logging interactions:

- Avoid including sensitive personal information unless necessary
- Don't record information the contact specifically asked to keep confidential
- Remember that all team members can view logs
- Consider your company's data retention and privacy policies
- Be professional in tone and content

## Measuring Networking Effectiveness

Interaction logs help you analyze your networking ROI:

- **Event follow-up rate** - What percentage of attendees get followed up with?
- **Conversion tracking** - Which events lead to meaningful business outcomes?
- **Response rates** - Who responds to follow-up and who doesn't?
- **Team activity** - Are all team members following up consistently?
- **Relationship depth** - Which contacts have rich, ongoing interaction histories?

Review interaction logs regularly to identify patterns and improve your networking strategy.
